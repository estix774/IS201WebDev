A Countdown of My Favorite Albums

As of April 12, 2024

Home Page About Me Most Recent Post

Top Ten

  1. Wish You Were Here, Pink Floyd
    • Only five tracks, the bookend tracks elevate the listener to another plane.
    • The title track is among the best that play as a stand alone.
  2. The Wall, Pink Floyd
    • Could be ranked 1, this Rock Opera is a double album with stand alone hits including Another Brick in the Wall pt. 2, Hey You, Comfortably Numb, and Run Like Hell.
  3. Aja, Steely Dan
    • Another short album, it blends soft rock and jazz, and features an all-time track, Deacon Blues.
  4. By The Way, Red Hot Chili Peppers
    • Arguably my favorite group, my favorite album that guitarist John Frusciante takes most ownership of
  5. Pet Sounds, The Beach Boys
    • The best of what The Beach Boys made comes together in this cohesive album.
  6. Thriller, Michael Jackson
    • A staple to anyone who appreciates music, this album features a three track run of Thriller, Beat It, and Billie Jean. It doesn't get much better.
  7. Animals, Pink Floyd
    • A commentary of Britain's political and working class, Roger Waters provides three cohesive, albeit unique middle tracks.
  8. What's Going On, Marvin Gaye
    • The best of the best when it comes to rhythm and blues.
  9. Rubber Soul, The Beatles
    • Of all the Beatles albums, this one has no misses for me, and features a personal favorite, Norweigan Wood.
  10. The Dark Side of the Moon, Pink Floyd
    • Widely regarded as the greatest album of all time.

Honorable Mentions

  • Abbey Road, The Beatles
  • The Getaway, Red Hot Chili Peppers
  • Can't Buy a Thrill, Steely Dan
  • Rodeo, Travis Scott
